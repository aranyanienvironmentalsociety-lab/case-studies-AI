Temporal Surge & Drivers

Data shows a cluster of attacks during the transition from the dry summer (April-May) to the pre-monsoon period. This correlates with two primary environmental pressures:

01

Hydrological Migration

Core water holes in NNTR dry up by late April, pushing tigers toward perennial canals and village ponds.

02

Territorial Displacement

Dominant males like "Jambhdi" taking over core territories force sub-adults and floaters into the buffer zones.
